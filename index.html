1. Circle of Competence

Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger

Know what you know and, more importantly, know what you do not know.

Stay within your circle for critical decisions. Expand it deliberately over time through study and practice. When you must act outside your circle, seek expert guidance.

2. Inversion

Charlie Munger via Carl Jacobi

Invert, always invert.

Instead of asking how to achieve success, ask what would guarantee failure -- then avoid those things. This reveals blind spots that forward thinking misses.

3. First Principles Thinking

Elon Musk via Aristotle

Boil things down to fundamental truths and reason up from there.

Break problems into their most basic components. Rebuild solutions from the ground up rather than reasoning by analogy.

Practice with scenarios

4. Margin of Safety

Benjamin Graham

The function of the margin of safety is to render forecasts unnecessary.

Build buffers into every decision. When your analysis says something is worth 100, do not pay more than 70. The margin protects against errors in judgment.

Read more principles

5. Antifragility

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Some things benefit from shocks; they thrive and grow when exposed to volatility.

Design your decisions to benefit from disorder. Use the barbell strategy: be very conservative in most areas and very aggressive in a few.
